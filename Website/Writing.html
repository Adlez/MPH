Jack

Jack set down Through the Marshland for the third time this morning. A boy of fourteen years, Jack was taller and stronger than others of his age, and unlike most boys he was very fond of literature. His father had been the king of the country Eluryh, a peaceful country where the land is mostly rolling plains with a large forest along it's south and eastern border and his mother, the only angel who stayed on Terra when the others had all left. Jack's hair is an unnatural blue reaching just past his neck and above his emerald eyes, his build lean with muscles defined from his martial training with the city guard. Of all the angelic children Jack is gifted with the most potent magical abilities, earning him the jealousy of the Hurtzva twins of Albion, even his mother is impressed by his mastery over fire. In his room you'll see a bed never made by his hands sitting atop a red and gold carpet. A black dresser containing his princely clothes, his vanity mirror (covered in dust), and wall to wall shelves filled with books detailing ages long past and both fictitious and true adventures, the later being his favourite.

Mima

Mima found herself standing in the crater she had made thousands of years ago while trying to take the sword of Eons. The dirt was black, bereft of life, her spell had backfired horribly, too much butter that time. Though ultimately a failure, it still impressed her that she had caused this massive hole of lifeless land to exist, The sword may have had something to do with I'll admit, but this is a damn fine hole. The first thing she did after recovering her staff, which was by no means an easy task, was attempt to track down the sword. Not knowing what else to do, she decided to start at the beginning again. So here she stood with the wind blowing through her hair, attempting to retrace the steps taken all those years ago.
“This is the wrong beginning.” The voice surprised her enough to cause her to jump. Cheap ass jump scares. She turned around to see a woman standing a few feet away from her. Her outerwear did not match the era she was in, long robes that back in Mima's day would have been considered royal. She could clearly see a body suit underneath, black with bright blue patterns. “I said you're at the wrong beginning, are you just going to stand there like a higga, oblivious to my words?” Who the hell does she think she is!? Does she have any idea who she's speaking to!? Mima approached the stranger, keeping herself composed all the while.
“I don't suppose you know where to start then?”
